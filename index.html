About Me

On January 25th, 1990; Chase Follett was born in the small, scenic town of Bryan, Ohio to Jeff and Debra Follett. Raised in the small, sleepy town; Chase attended the city of Bryan schools. As a youth, he played many different sports such as football, baseball, soccer and roller hockey; all helping him develop skills that would be beneficial to him as he progressed through life. From high school, Chase attended Ohio University where he graduated with a degree in Political Science and Philosophy. After college graduation, he moved to Columbus, Ohio from 2013-2017. In Columbus, he worked with many local non-profit organizations to help teach water safety to young swimmers and trained many young lifeguards on the importance of lifeguarding and water safety. In September of 2017, he was offered a position with another non-profit in Colorado and made the move to Lafayette, Colorado where he currently resides.

In his free time, Chase is an fitness fanatic. He spends 5 to 6 day's a week training, whether that be in the gym or on the road. Chase is avid weight lifter; he began resistance training at the age of 17 and has been passionate about spreading his wealth of knowledge on the subject to anyone whose interested. Besides resistance training, Chase loves to train for 5k's, half-marathons and full marathons. He has competed in over 25 5k's, 4 half-marathons and 4 full-marathons. While never competeing at an elite level, Chase continues to enjoy long-distance running and the training that goes into it.

Beyond fitness, Chase enjoys playing guitar, listening to music, preaching the Gosphel of Metallica, hiking, relaxing with friends, and a good brewery or moutain town bar that always has some amazing beer and one-of-a-kind food. Chase loves the Ohio State Buckeye's, Pittsburgh Steelers and is an enthusiastic motorsports fan with a passion for motorsports history. Chase has an extensive knowledge of Nascar and Nascar history, of which, no one ever cares to listen or finds interesting besides Chase and possibly his mother who has to listen to him when he talks about it
